Experimental Study

This study is to help us understand better on the perception of categorical scatterplots. To attend this study, it is required that you are aged between 18 to 65 and with normal color vision. Before the study, you will first be asked to provide basic information and pass the color blindness test. In the main study, you will be asked 45 questions. The total duration should be less than 20 minutes.

For each question you will see a scatterplot. In a scatterplot, each color represents one category. You will be asked to select the category which has the highest average value (based on the y-values). An example chart is shown below. For this example scatterplot, there are two categories represented by the colors blue and orange. In this case, the answer is the blue that has the highest average of y-values.

Each question has a time limitation of 15 seconds. Once you select your answer, please click the next button to move onto the next question. The first question will be a practice question.
